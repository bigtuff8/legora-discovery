4 The Adjudication Pressure Cooker

From my research, adjudication seems like the most intense pressure point for a solo construction lawyer. The timelines are brutal:

Day 0: Notice of Adjudication served
Day 7: Referral Notice submitted (with ALL evidence)
Day 14: Response typically due (if responding party)
Day 28: Decision (extendable to Day 42 with consent)
The solo challenge
When this kicks off, you have to drop everything for 7-14 days. Hundreds of pages of submissions, evidence, contract analysis, legal arguments -- and all other client work stops. This is where smart use of AI could make the biggest difference.

7 AI & Compliance (Quick Check)

The SRA hasn't created AI-specific rules yet, but existing professional standards apply. The key points are: you're personally responsible for all AI output, client data should only go into secure tools (Legora is fine), and you should be transparent with clients about AI use.

Legora's security stack (ISO 27001, SOC 2, GDPR, AES-256 encryption, tenant isolation) should satisfy SRA requirements. That's one of its advantages over using free AI tools.
